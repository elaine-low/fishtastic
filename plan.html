Veterinary Services

  • What are the veterinary hours?

Monday to Friday from 8am to 9pm. On Saturdays we are open from 9am until 5pm and we are open Sunday from 10am until 2pm.

  • Do I need to make an appointment?

Generally patients are seen by appointments but we can accommodate emergencies without an appointment. If possible please call ahead of time and make an appointment with us.

  • What form of payment do you accept?

We accept Paypal, Visa Debit/Credit Cards and Mastercard Debit/Credit Cards.Payment is due when services are performed or when your pet leaves the vet. A down payment is required before emergency treatment is provided.

  • What if I cannot leave work to bring my pets to their veterinary appointments?

You may drop off your pet with us in the morning and your pet will be seen during the day. Once everything is done we will call you afterwards to pick up your pet anytime from when we call until 9pm on weekdays, 5pm on Saturdays and 2pm on Sunday. There is no additional charge for drop-off service. Animals not picked up will stay overnight and a boarding fee will be added.

  • What kind of animals can you take care of?

We provide veterinary care for puppies, kittens, dogs, cats, hamsters, rabbits, guinea pigs and fishes.

  • What should I do if my pet gets extremely ill or injured after hours?

Call the clinic and your call will be transferred to one of our veterinarians on-call. If emergency care is necessary you are required to make a down payment before we can provide care for your pet. Full payment is due when your pet is ready to go home.

  • How often should I take my pet to the vet?

Every pet has unique medical needs, so you and your vet should discuss an ongoing healthcare plan for your animal, as your pet may have a condition that requires relatively frequent visits to treat properly. However, generally speaking, we recommend bringing a healthy pet in at least once a year for a wellness visit, to make sure he or she is up to date on his/her shots and that everything is functioning as it should.

  • How do I know if my pet is sick?

Sometimes animals hide their illnesses or injuries so it is important to pay attention and be aware of the signs of symptoms that require immediate urgent emergency care. If you suspect your pet is unwell please do not hesitate to call us. Early veterinary attention can prevent serious illnesses from progressing and reduce the cost of care.

  • What age can I have my pet spayed or neutered?

Spaying or neutering can be done at approximately 6 months of age. Your pet is given an exam prior to surgery to help determine whether your pet is healthy enough to undergo the surgical procedure. Current vaccinations are required at the time of surgery. Also a pre-anesthetic blood screen is performed prior to undergoing anesthesia and surgery.

  • How long do sutures stay in after my pet’s surgery?

Procedures involving sutures require them to be removed in 10 – 14 days following the surgery.

  • What breed of dogs need grooming?

All dogs need their coat and nails tended to, but some dogs require much more grooming than others. Some breeds that require frequent grooming are Poodles, Shih Tzus and Lhasa Apsos, Bichon Frises, Yorkshire Terriers, Schnauzers, and West Highland Terriers.

  • Do you board pets?

We have boarding facilities for dogs within our clinic. We encourage you to bring your pet’s own food. If your pet is on any medication, please bring it with you when you drop your pet off.
